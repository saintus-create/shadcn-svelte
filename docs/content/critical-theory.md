# Critical Theory Documentation - Sidebar Structure

## Sidebar Option 1: Overview
**Content:**
- **Definition**: Critical theory is a social philosophy that critiques and transforms society by revealing power structures and ideologies that dominate and oppress.
- **Core Mission**: Liberation through enlightenment; differs from traditional theory by being actively normative and practical rather than merely descriptive.
- **Historical Origins**: Institute for Social Research (Frankfurt School), founded 1923; developed in response to fascism, Stalinism, and the failures of traditional Marxism.
- **Key Distinction**: Traditional theory explains society; critical theory seeks to change it.

---

## Sidebar Option 2: Key Thinkers
**Content:**
### *Foundational Figures (1920s-1970s)*
- **Theodor W. Adorno**: Culture industry, negative dialectics, aesthetic theory (*Dialectic of Enlightenment*, *Minima Moralia*)
- **Max Horkheimer**: Instrumental reason critique, traditional vs. critical theory (*Eclipse of Reason*)
- **Herbert Marcuse**: One-dimensional society, erotic liberation, New Left influence (*One-Dimensional Man*)
- **Walter Benjamin**: Aura, mechanical reproduction, historical materialism (*Work of Art in Age of Mechanical Reproduction*)
- **Erich Fromm**: Authoritarian personality, Marxist humanism (*Escape from Freedom*)

### *Second Generation*
- **Jürgen Habermas**: Public sphere, communicative rationality, discourse ethics (*Structural Transformation of Public Sphere*)
- **Axel Honneth**: Recognition theory, social freedom (*Struggle for Recognition*)

### *Contemporary & Interdisciplinary*
- **Nancy Fraser**: Justice as redistribution & recognition
- **Judith Butler**: Gender performativity, queer theory
- **Slavoj Žižek**: Lacanian Marxism, ideology critique
- **Postcolonial**: Edward Said, Gayatri Spivak, Homi Bhabha
- **Critical Race**: Kimberlé Crenshaw, Derrick Bell, bell hooks

## Sidebar Option 3: Core Concepts
**Content:**
### *Ideology Critique*
- **Culture Industry**: Commercial mass media produces standardized culture that pacifies and controls
- **False Consciousness**: Ideology obscures true social conditions and interests
- **Reification**: Social relations transformed into thing-like relations (Lukács)

### *Reason & Rationality*
- **Instrumental Reason**: Means-end thinking dominates all life spheres; efficiency becomes irrational
- **Dialectic of Enlightenment**: Reason regresses into myth when instrumentalized (Horkheimer & Adorno)

### *Communication & Recognition*
- **Public Sphere**: Space for rational-critical debate (Habermas)
- **Communicative Action**: Interaction oriented toward mutual understanding
- **Recognition**: Social struggles are fights for mutual recognition (Honneth)

### *Culture & Aesthetics*
- **Aura**: Unique authenticity destroyed by mechanical reproduction (Benjamin)
- **Negative Dialectics**: Philosophy that refuses positive synthesis (Adorno)

## Sidebar Option 4: Schools & Movements
**Content:**
- **First Frankfurt School**: Dialectical materialism, psychoanalytic Marxism, culture critique
- **Habermasian School**: Communicative turn, discourse theory, deliberative democracy
- **Poststructuralist Critical Theory**: Foucauldian power analysis, deconstruction, anti-foundationalism
- **Postcolonial Critical Theory**: Subaltern studies, Orientalism critique, decolonization
- **Critical Race Theory**: Intersectionality, whiteness as property, counter-storytelling
- **Feminist Critical Theory**: Gender construction, standpoint epistemology, reproductive labor
- **Ecocritical Theory**: Domination of nature, environmental justice, Anthropocene critique

## Sidebar Option 6: Key Terms A-Z
**Content:**
- **Alienation**: Separation from species-being (Marx)
- **Aura**: Authenticity in art (Benjamin)
- **Authoritarian Personality**: Fascist character structure (Adorno)
- **Colonization of Lifeworld**: System intrusion into personal life (Habermas)
- **Culture Industry**: Mass media as ideological control
- **False Needs**: Manufactured desires (Marcuse)
- **Hegemony**: Ideological leadership (Gramsci)
- **Intersectionality**: Overlapping oppressions (Crenshaw)
- **Performative**: Speech acts constituting reality (Butler)
- **Reification**: Social relations as things
- **Subaltern**: Marginalized outside hegemony (Spivak)
- **Technocratic Consciousness**: Efficiency as sole value

## Sidebar Option 8: Debates & Criticisms
**Content:**
### *Internal Critiques*
- **Habermas vs. First Generation**: Rejection of totalizing critique; communicative action alternative
- **Recognition vs. Redistribution**: Fraser-Honneth debate on justice priorities
- **Poststructuralist Challenge**: Anti-foundationalism and the problem of relativism
- **Identity vs. Class**: Tension between intersectionality and class analysis

### *External Criticisms*
- **Conservative**: "Cultural Marxism" narrative; anti-Western bias
- **Analytical**: Too speculative; lacks empirical falsifiability
- **Decolonial**: Eurocentric; ignores non-Western thought
- **Pragmatist**: Impractical negativity; offers no constructive alternatives

### *Current Frontiers*
- Digital culture and algorithmic power
- Global capitalism and financial crises
- Climate crisis and ecological domination
- Platform labor and AI automation
- Epistemic injustice and standpoint theory

---

## Sidebar Option 9: Methodology
**Content:**
### *Core Methods*
- **Immanent Critique**: Revealing contradictions within society's own norms
- **Ideology Critique**: Unmasking interests behind universal claims
- **Negative Dialectics**: Refusing premature positive alternatives (Adorno)
- **Genealogical Method**: Foucauldian history of the present
- **Discourse Analysis**: Power relations in language and communication
- **Participatory Action Research**: Collaborative inquiry with marginalized groups

### *Research Principles*
- Normative commitment to emancipation
- Reflexivity about researcher positionality
- Totality: connecting parts to whole social system
- Historicization: situating phenomena in historical context
